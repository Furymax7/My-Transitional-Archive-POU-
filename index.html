ARCHIVE_01: German-Radio

Telefunken was a German radio and television producer, founded in Berlin in 1903 as a joint venture between Siemens & Halske and the Allgemeine Elektrizitäts-Gesellschaft.

Prior to World War I, the company set up the first world-wide network of communications and was the first in the world to sell electronic televisions with cathode ray tubes, in Germany in 1934.

Device type Intermediate frequency Year of manufacture
Radio with additional clock and alarm clock, Timer 460 kHz, 10.7 MHz 1988

ARCHIVE_02: IMAC G3

The iMac G3 is an iconic all-in-one Macintosh personal computer sold by Apple from 1998 to 2003.

Designed by Jony Ive, it featured a translucent, colorful, teardrop-shaped case, a 15-inch CRT display, and pioneered the widespread adoption of USB while abandoning floppy drives

Design Processor Memory-Storage Graphics
Translucent, candy-colored, teardrop-shaped plastic case. 233 MHz PowerPC 750 (G3). 32 MB SDRAM (upgradable), 4GB IDE hard drive. ATI Rage IIc (2MB VRAM) or Rage Pro Turbo (6MB VRAM).

ARCHIVE_03: TapeRecorder

The SLV-ED4 is an entirely standard piece of late stage analog technology. The 4 head system makes it functionally adequate for basic playback tasks. The Sapphire Tape Cleaner provides a marginal layer of physical protection for the heads against standard dust. It is primarily a marketing term designed to reassure consumers, and it absolutely does not replace proper tape maintenance, manual cleaning, or controlled storage.

Head Configuration System Compatibility Supplementary Features
4 rotary heads. Multi system (can handel typically PAL and NTSC) Adaptive Picture Control, Trinitron TV Syncroplay, Audio Dubbing, and an Easy Timer recording function.

ARCHIVE_04: CNC

The Roland Modela MDX-40 is a compact 4-axis desktop milling machine designed for Subtractive Rapid Prototyping (SRP), featuring a 305x305x105 mm working area. It is commonly used for, and ideal for high-precision modeling with wax, wood, acrylic, and epoxy resins, often used in studios for prototypes and small objects.

System Type Performance Software & Control
4-axis desktop CNC (rotary axis optional). Spindle speed of 4,500 to 15,000 rpm with amx resolution of 0.002mm/step. Uses VPanel to control the machine and commonly operates with Modela Player 4 for generating toolpaths from STL files.
